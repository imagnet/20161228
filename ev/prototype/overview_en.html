Object

The purpose of NCIS is that to provide the Korean chemical inventories and information about toxicity, hazard, regulatory etc. in the form of database for industries, laboratories and the public.

Services

  • - Update and linkage of hazard/risk data and regulation information in Korea and abroad
  • - Focusing on the construction of a database of chemical inventories and hazard information, using the chemical data available domestically
  • - Providing the list and regulation information of chemicals for chemical manufacturers and importers to confirm the domestic registration status of them
  • - Providing the OECD SIDS initial risk assessment report, the safety assessment report of Korean existing chemicals, the toxicity examination report of new chemicals, etc.
  • - Providing the lists of existing chemicals, toxic chemicals, observational chemicals, restricted chemicals or banned chemicals, accident precaution chemicals, etc.
  • - Providing the lists of international convention substances (OECD HPV chemicals, persistent organic pollutants, chemicals under the Rotterdam Convention, etc.)

Services

Establishment of initial foundation(’05)

  • - Established the National Chemicals Information System (http://ncis.nier.go.kr)
  • - Created a database of the existing chemicals information and developed a search program.
  • - Launched a substance search service on January 1. 2006, when a revised bill of the Toxic Chemicals Control Act came effect.

Full operation (‘06~present)

  • - Adding data by collecting hazard and risk information from overseas entities (OECD, USA and EU) : OECD SIDS, EPA risk report and EU risk report
  • - Conduction a safely test of chemicals whose toxic information is not prepared yet and created a database of their production information
  • - Creating a database of the toxicity examination report of new chemicals according to the Toxic Chemicals Control Act
  • - Creating a database of contents relating to the Globally Harmonized System of Classification and Labeling of Chemicals (GHS)
